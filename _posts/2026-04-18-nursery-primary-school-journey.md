
#  My First Classroom Experience

I still remember walking into my nursery classroom for the first time. The colorful walls, the small chairs, the alphabet posters - everything felt so big and new. I was probably nervous, maybe even cried a little, but that small room became my first window into the world of learning.

##  Nursery Years (Age 3-5)

### What I Learned
- **The Alphabet** - Singing the ABC song until it got stuck in my head
- **Numbers 1-20** - Counting fingers, toys, everything around me
- **Shapes and Colors** - Red circles, blue squares, yellow triangles
- **Social Skills** - Sharing toys, making friends, waiting for my turn

### My Favorite Activities
Drawing and coloring were my favorites. I'd spend hours with crayons, trying to draw anything I saw - cars, trees, my family. Looking back, those early drawings were my first form of creative expression.

### The Teacher Who Shaped Me
**Madam Malim Ndondo** - she was tricky but in a good way. She had this way of making learning feel like a game. What made her special was how she treated me and my brother Junior. She genuinely liked us and always made sure we were paying attention. Even when she was being strict, you could tell she cared. Looking back now, I realize she saw something in us that maybe we didn't see in ourselves yet.

##  Primary School Years (Grade 1-7)

Primary school is where things got real. Suddenly, learning wasn't just about play - it was about reading, writing, mathematics, and understanding the world.

### Grade 1-3: Learning the Basics

**Reading and Writing**
I remember the day I read my first full sentence without help. The feeling of accomplishment was incredible. From struggling with letter sounds to reading small storybooks - this was my first major academic milestone.

**Mathematics**
Addition and subtraction became my friends (sometimes enemies). Multiplication tables? Those took practice, lots of it.

**Science**
"Why is the sky blue?" "How do plants grow?" My curiosity started here. Every answer led to five more questions.

### Grade 4-5: Building Confidence

**Subjects I Loved**
- **Mathematics** - Something about solving problems clicked with me
- **Science** - Experiments and explanations fascinated me
- **Drama** - This became one of my favorite activities. Getting on stage, playing different characters, making people laugh - it taught me confidence and how to express myself

**Subjects I Struggled With**
**English** - This was my biggest challenge. The grammar rules, the spellings, the pronunciations - nothing came easy. I had to work twice as hard as others. But struggling with English taught me something valuable: difficulty doesn't mean impossibility. I kept practicing, kept making mistakes, and kept learning.

**Extracurricular Activities**
- **Football** - I loved playing. The field was where I could forget about everything else and just run, pass, and score. Football taught me teamwork, persistence, and how to handle both winning and losing
- **Drama** - Performing on stage was magical. Drama club became my creative outlet and helped me build confidence that I still use today when presenting projects or speaking in class

### Grade 6-7: Preparing for Secondary

These were the years I started taking learning more seriously. The work got harder, expectations increased, and I began thinking about what came next.

### Young Love

There's someone I still haven't forgotten to this day - **Magambe, also known as Leisa**. She was my girlfriend back in primary school. Young love is different - innocent, pure, and unforgettable. Walking to class together, sharing notes, stealing glances across the classroom. Those memories stay with you. Even now, working on data science and machine learning projects, I sometimes think about those simple days and smile. Leisa, if you ever read this - I hope you're doing well.

### What I Wanted To Be

Back then, I didn't dream of being a software engineer or data scientist. I wanted to be a **teacher**. Just like my father. Seeing him in the classroom, how students respected him, how he made a difference in young lives - that inspired me. Life took me in a different direction towards technology and engineering, but that desire to teach and share knowledge never left. Maybe that's why I'm writing this blog - to teach, to share, to help others on their journey.

##  Key Lessons From These Early Years

Looking back at nursery and primary school, here's what I learned that still matters today:

1. **Curiosity is a superpower** - Asking "why" led me to where I am now, analyzing life expectancy data and building prediction models

2. **Struggling with a subject doesn't mean you're stupid** - English was hard for me, but I kept going. Now I'm writing blog posts in English

3. **Teachers who believe in you change everything** - Madam Malim Ndondo saw something in me and my brother Junior

4. **Football and drama taught me skills textbooks couldn't** - Teamwork, confidence, handling pressure, performing under observation

5. **Young love creates lasting memories** - Some people stay in your heart, even when life moves on

##  Memories That Stay With Me

- **Madam Malim Ndondo** - Her tricky teaching style and how she genuinely cared about me and Junior
- **Playing football** during break time with friends, the joy of scoring a goal, the disappointment of missing
- **Performing in drama** - the nervousness before going on stage, the adrenaline, the applause
- **Leisa (Magambe)** - my first girlfriend, someone I still remember today
- **Wanting to be a teacher** like my father before technology captured my heart

##  Reflection

I was just a kid with a backpack, a football, and big dreams. I struggled with English, fell in love too young, played football until my legs hurt, and wanted to be just like my father - a teacher.

I never imagined back then that I'd end up in Software Engineering, working on machine learning projects, analyzing life expectancy data, building IoT systems, and writing code in multiple programming languages.

But every experience mattered. The struggle with English made me persistent. Football taught me teamwork. Drama gave me confidence. Madam Malim Ndondo showed me what good teaching looks like. And wanting to be a teacher? That desire to help others learn is exactly why I'm writing these blog posts today.
